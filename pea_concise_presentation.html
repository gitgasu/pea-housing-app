The Problem

Housing insecurity in New York City is a complex, multi-faceted challenge affecting thousands of residents. Current resources are fragmented, overwhelming, and often inaccessible to those who need them most.

Critical Pain Points

  • Information Overload: Too many disconnected resources and websites
  • System Complexity: Navigating government programs requires expertise
  • Landlord Discrimination: Voucher holders face systemic rejection
  • Hidden Costs: Broker fees can equal 15% of annual rent
  • Lack of Human Support: Critical need for guidance and emotional support
"Don't make me more confused than I already am."
- Mr. L, Emergency Housing Seeker

Design Principles

Trauma-Informed Design
Respectful of vulnerable populations experiencing crisis. Never patronizing, always empowering.
User-Centered Approach
Every design decision validated by research findings. Real user quotes guide the design.
Accessibility First
WCAG 2.1 AA compliance, plain language (8th grade level), mobile-first, minimum 44x44px touch targets.
Progressive Disclosure
Show basic information first, details on demand. Reduce cognitive load while providing depth when needed.
Human-Centered Technology
Technology supports, humans empower. 24/7 access to real people, not just algorithms.
Radical Simplicity
"Don't make me more confused" - Every feature must reduce cognitive load, not add to it.

MVP Features (Phase 1)

1. Personalized Action Plans
Validation: 100% of participants needed clear, sequential guidance
Features: Step-by-step checklists tailored to user situation, progress tracking, time estimates, plain language
Impact: Reduces overwhelm, provides clarity, builds confidence
2. 24/7 Human Support Hub
Validation: 100% of participants needed human connection
Features: Four channels (chat, phone, video, SMS), always accessible, response time indicators
Impact: Provides emotional support, expert guidance, reduces isolation
3. Smart Suggestions Engine
Validation: 100% wanted proactive, personalized guidance
Features: AI-powered recommendations, contextual tips, deadline alerts
Impact: Fills knowledge gaps, prevents missed opportunities
4. Housing Search & Filter
Validation: Critical for voucher holders and cost-conscious renters
Features: No-fee filter, voucher-accepting landlords, acceptance rates, cost calculators
Impact: Saves thousands in broker fees, reduces discrimination

Design Solutions

Persona-Specific Design Strategies

For Mr. L: Radical Simplicity

Dashboard shows only 3-4 urgent actions. Large buttons. Minimal text. Prominent "Get Help Now" button. Progressive disclosure of complexity.

For Ms. Ellis: Strategic Information

Prominent voucher filter. Landlord acceptance rates visible. Rights information accessible. Success stories for motivation. Tools to build landlord relationships.

For Mr. Patterson: Financial Transparency

No-fee listings prominently featured. Cost calculators and comparisons. Savings tracking. Hidden cost warnings. Neighborhood affordability data.

Universal Features Across All Personas

  • Human Support: All personas need access to real people for guidance
  • Transparency: Clear information about costs, processes, and options
  • Trust: System must be reliable, honest, and advocate for the user
  • Mobile Access: All users have smartphones, prefer mobile experience
  • Empowerment: Want to feel capable and confident, not patronized
